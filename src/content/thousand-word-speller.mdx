How can we effectively convey and express the images within our minds? Especially when our means of communication, either because verbal or motor impairment, are limited.

This is a P300 based speller with a board made up of images. This method uses generative image and language models to create new pictures in real-time based on the user's current selections.

This project obtained second place at the Spring 2022 IEEE BR41N.io Hackathon.

## Role and Tools

I programmed the interface with ChatGPT and Stable Diffusion's APIs, contributed to the design of the board, and system testing.

## Background

This project aimed to find a simple way to communicate mental imagery directly from a BCI without requiring any skill or motor input. Considering that the P300 protocol has been widely studied and optimized [[1]](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2022.1077717), we used an experimentally validated speller [[2]](https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-22375-4_68) provided by g.Tec for their BR41N.io hackathon.

As a future line of research, we would like to look into the use of this system for patients with aphasia [[3]](https://doi.org/10.1006/ijhc.2002.1018), [[4]](https://doi.org/10.3389/fnhum.2016.00547), while also optimizing it to adapt its image selection in real-time for more specific artworks.
